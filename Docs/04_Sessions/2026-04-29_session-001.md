# 2026-04-29 Session 001

## Goal
Address App Review rejection under Guidelines 5.1.1(i) and 5.1.2(i) by disclosing third-party AI data processing in app, updating the public privacy policy, and preparing a reviewer response.

## Context
Apple Review said the app appears to share personal data with a third-party AI service without clearly explaining what data is sent, who receives it, and asking permission before sharing.

- `Life Narattor/ContentView.swift`
- `site/privacy/index.html`

- `Docs/05_Changes/Change-219-ai-processing-consent-and-review-response.md`
- `Docs/VERIFICATION_BACKLOG.md`

## Implementation
- Added `privacy.hasConsentedToAIProcessing` app storage state.
- Changed the root gate so users must have both `app.hasSeenPrivacyIntro` and `privacy.hasConsentedToAIProcessing` before entering the main app.
- Rewrote the first-launch privacy screen into a clear AI processing consent screen.
- The screen now states:
  - local-first default storage
  - what data may be sent for AI processing
  - AI provider recipients: Life Narrator backend proxy, OpenAI, and Volcano Engine/Doubao for speech transcription
  - use limitation: requested transcription, assistant, organization, review, splitting, or tagging only
  - no advertising tracking, no sale of content, and no upstream provider key exposure
- Updated the public privacy policy page with the same disclosure categories.
- Added a ready-to-paste App Review response draft.
- Updated beta review notes with the new privacy boundary.
